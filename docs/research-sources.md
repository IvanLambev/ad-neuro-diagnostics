# Research Sources Index

This file keeps the research references in one place so we can reuse them later in product documentation, landing pages, investor material, and internal notes.

## Core Advertising and Neuroforecasting Sources

### 1. Venkatraman et al.

- Title: *Predicting Advertising Success Beyond Traditional Measures: New Insights from Neurophysiological Methods and Market Response Modeling*
- Link: [PDF](https://assets.tina.io/a371bbc3-e9fe-472a-a32b-f55a8990b0bc/Venkatraman%20et%20al.%20Predicting%20Advertising%20Success%20Beyond%20Traditional%20Measures-%20New%20Insights%20from%20Neurophysiological%20Methods%20and%20Market%20Response%20Modeling.pdf)
- Why it matters:
  - Shows that neurophysiological measures can add incremental forecasting value beyond traditional ad testing metrics.
  - Useful support for the idea that brain-derived signals can help rank or compare ads more effectively than self-report alone.

### 2. Genevsky and Knutson

- Title: *Neuroforecasting Aggregate Choice*
- Link: [SAGE article](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0963721417737877)
- Why it matters:
  - Good high-level review of neuroforecasting.
  - Supports careful positioning: some neural processes generalize better than others, so product claims should stay comparative and evidence-based.

### 3. Genevsky et al.

- Title: *Neuroforecasting reveals generalizable components of choice*
- Link: [PNAS Nexus article](https://academic.oup.com/pnasnexus/article-abstract/4/2/pgaf029/8016018)
- Why it matters:
  - Supports the idea that some early affective neural signals can predict aggregate outcomes more robustly than simple behavior-only forecasts.
  - Useful for our planned hook and early-lift diagnostics.

## Trust, Commerce, and Purchase Friction Sources

### 4. Casado-Aranda et al.

- Title: *Consumer Processing of Online Trust Signals: A Neuroimaging Study*
- Link: [ScienceDirect article](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1094996819300520)
- Why it matters:
  - Helpful support for building trust-close or friction-reduction proxy signals.
  - Relevant for CTA, offer, checkout-style confidence, and end-card interpretation.

## Message Impact and Content Processing Sources

### 5. Wang et al.

- Title: *Content Matters: Neuroimaging Investigation of Brain and Behavioral Impact of Televised Anti-Tobacco Public Service Announcements*
- Link: [PubMed entry](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23616548/)
- Why it matters:
  - Supports the claim that message content and structure can change both neural response and downstream impact.
  - Useful for event-aligned analysis and explanation quality.

### 6. Nature Reviews Neuroscience summary

- Title: *Evaluating ads with fMRI*
- Link: [Nature page](https://www.nature.com/articles/nrn3521)
- Why it matters:
  - Useful as a concise high-level reference when explaining why ad testing and brain-response analysis are connected.
  - Best used as context, not as a primary technical source.

## How we should use these sources

- Use them to support claims about comparative forecasting, creative diagnosis, trust/risk signals, memory, and attention.
- Do not use them to claim that our current system can directly read purchase intent from one exact brain region.
- Position the product as a stronger benchmark and insight layer on top of TRIBE, not as a literal medical-grade brain-reading tool.
